SECTION 5: WHAT'S MISSING

EVIDENCE NEEDED TO PROVE IT

To move from "pattern" to "proof," we need specific evidence.

KNOWLEDGE

What did decision-makers know, and when?

  • Governor's office briefings on fraud
  • MDE/DHS internal communications
  • FBI warning documentation
  • Who was told what, when?

DECISIONS

Who approved policies that enabled fraud?

  • Who waived verification requirements?
  • Who approved problematic providers?
  • Who decided not to fire anyone?
  • What were the approval chains?

CONNECTIONS

Who benefits and how are they connected?

  • Ownership of fraud organizations
  • Family/business relationships
  • Same people across multiple programs?
  • Political donation flows

MONEY TRAIL

Where did the money actually go?

  • Bank records of organizations
  • Real estate purchases
  • Wire transfers out of state/country
  • Shell company structures

WHAT WOULD PROVE THE THEORY?

Internal memo showing knowledge of fraud before public → Proves cover-up
Decision document waiving verification, signed by official → Proves enabling
Same owners across multiple fraud schemes → Proves coordination
Donations flowing back from beneficiaries → Proves motive
Communication between officials and fraudsters → Proves conspiracy
